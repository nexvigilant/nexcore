## PREAMBLE — READ THIS FIRST

NexVigilant exists to make pharmacovigilance accessible to intelligent
beginners. The tools published here — signal detection algorithms, causality
decision trees, case processing wizards, and AI-assisted PV workflows — were
built from real-world pharmacovigilance experience, including years of
enterprise PV infrastructure design that large organizations chose not to
pursue. That institutional knowledge is now available to you for free,
under one condition: you must not use it to run a commercial business.

### What You May Do Under This License

- Learn pharmacovigilance concepts using these tools as a study aid
- Conduct academic research and publish findings (with attribution)
- Build personal projects to deepen your own PV skills
- Teach PV concepts in a classroom, workshop, or online course (non-commercial)
- Experiment, prototype, and explore — as long as no money changes hands
- Contribute improvements back to the community

### What You May NOT Do Under This License

- Use these tools in any for-profit business operation
- Integrate these tools into a commercial product or SaaS platform
- Use these tools to process adverse events for a client under a commercial
  contract, even if the tool itself is not the product being sold
- Allow a for-profit entity (including your employer) to benefit from your
  use of these tools in the course of your employment or contract work
- Sublicense, sell, or transfer these tools to another party for commercial
  use
- Use these tools to provide pharmacovigilance services for compensation

### Who This License Is For

Students, PharmD candidates, academic researchers, independent safety
scientists, volunteer safety monitors for non-profit organizations, and
individuals learning PV on their own time. If your use generates revenue,
creates commercial value for a for-profit entity, or supports a business
operation — you need a commercial license.

### Commercial Licensing

If your use case falls outside these boundaries, commercial licenses are
