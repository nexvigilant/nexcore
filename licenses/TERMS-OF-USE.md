## Implementation Notes for Matthew

The five documents above work as an integrated system. Here is how they interlock and what you need to finalize before publication.

**What requires your attorney's review before going live:**

1. The BSL 1.1 template in LICENSE-COMMERCIAL.md requires you to specify your state of incorporation in Section 10.2 and Section 9.2 of the Terms. The governing law and jurisdiction clauses in TERMS-OF-USE.md Sections 9.2 and 9.3 have bracketed placeholders for the same reason.

2. The TERMS-OF-USE.md limitation of liability in Section 7.3 caps NexVigilant's liability at USD $100. This is a standard approach for free-tier software but should be confirmed against your jurisdiction's consumer protection minimums, particularly for EU users.

3. The class action waiver in Section 9.4 is enforceable in many US jurisdictions but not all, and is not enforceable in the EU or UK. The carve-out language is present but your counsel should confirm it is adequate.

4. GDPR compliance for Section 5 depends on where your infrastructure is hosted. The SCCs reference in Section 5.5 needs to be backed by an actual SCC addendum if you are processing EEA user data on US servers.

**What you need to create operationally before launch:**

- The email addresses referenced throughout: `licensing@`, `enterprise@`, `legal@`, `privacy@`, `abuse@`, `support@` — these all need to route somewhere
- A public page at `https://nexvigilant.com/releases` tracking version publication dates (required for the BSL Change Date mechanism to function correctly)
- A sub-processors list at `https://nexvigilant.com/sub-processors` (required for GDPR transparency)
- The onboarding questionnaire affirmative representation referenced in TERMS-OF-USE.md Section 3.2

**The domain block list cadence:** The list in ALLOWED-DOMAINS.md covers approximately 60 named pharmaceutical and CRO companies. It will need quarterly updates as you discover new domains in the manual review queue. The catch-all in Section 3 handles everything not explicitly listed, so gaps are contained — they just generate manual review tickets rather than unauthorized access.

**The strongest legal protection in this framework** is the combination of (a) the affirmative representation at registration, (b) the anti-circumvention clause in TERMS-OF-USE.md Section 3, and (c) the audit rights in Section 4. Domain gating is a friction mechanism, not a security perimeter. The ToS is the enforceable instrument.
